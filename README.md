# sp500-market-valuation-analysis
Exploratory analysis of S&P 500 market valuation using Python, Yahoo Finance API, and interactive visualizations.# What Drives Market Valuation in the S&P 500?

## Project Overview

This project explores the financial characteristics associated with market valuation across S&P 500 companies using live financial data retrieved through the Yahoo Finance API (`yfinance`).

The objective was to investigate whether financial metrics such as revenue, net income, and valuation multiples (P/E ratio) are associated with market capitalization while also examining how market value is distributed across sectors.

Rather than building a predictive model, this project focuses on exploratory data analysis (EDA) and financial storytelling through data visualization.

## Business Question

**What financial characteristics are most closely associated with market valuation in the S&P 500?**

## Data Sources

- Wikipedia (S&P 500 company list)
- Yahoo Finance API (`yfinance`)

## Technologies

- Python
- Pandas
- NumPy
- Matplotlib
- Plotly
- Jupyter Notebook

## Visualizations

- Top Companies by Market Capitalization
- Top Companies by Revenue
- Top Companies by Net Income
- Revenue vs. Market Capitalization
- Net Income vs. Market Capitalization
- P/E Ratio vs. Market Capitalization
- Market Capitalization by Sector
- Interactive Treemap

## Key Findings

- Revenue alone does not explain market valuation.
- Net income shows a stronger relationship with market capitalization than revenue.
- Investor expectations, reflected in P/E ratios, also contribute to company valuation.
- Technology and Communication Services account for the largest share of the S&P 500's total market capitalization.
